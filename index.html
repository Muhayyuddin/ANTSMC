Paper Abstract

Overview

Unmanned Surface Vehicles (USVs) are increasingly deployed in ocean engineering applications such as hydrographic surveying, environmental monitoring, and maritime security. Achieving accurate path following in realistic ocean environments remains challenging due to nonlinear vessel dynamics, environmental disturbances, and underactuation arising from the absence of direct sway control. This paper proposes an Adaptive Nonlinear Terminal Sliding Mode Controller (ANTSMC) for a three-degree-of-freedom (3-DOF) underactuated USV subject to wind, wave, and current disturbances. The controller integrates a nonlinear terminal sliding surface with a power-rate reaching law and a leakage-regulated adaptive switching gain that adjusts robustness online without a disturbance observer. A benchmark evaluation was conducted across six controllers (LQR, SMC, ASMC, ADRC, FNTSMC, and ANTSMC), four path geometries, and three WMO sea states while accounting for realistic sensor noise and parametric uncertainty. The results show that ANTSMC achieves cross-track accuracy comparable to SMC under calm sea conditions, while demonstrating stronger robustness as environmental disturbances increase. Independent validation using the MBZIRC physics-based maritime simulator further confirms stable path-following performance across different path geometries. A Monte Carlo study verifies robustness under expanded parametric and disturbance uncertainties.

Control Design

ANTSMC Architecture

An observer-free adaptive framework integrating three synergistic mechanisms for robust underactuated path following.

⚡
Nonlinear Terminal
Sliding Surface
Fractional power (α = 0.6) amplifies small errors by up to 2.5× for tighter steady-state tracking with finite-time convergence.
🎯
Power-Rate
Reaching Law
Exponent p = 0.85 accelerates convergence near the sliding surface while moderating growth for large deviations.
🔄
Adaptive
Switching Gain
Error-proportional leakage strengthens robustness under disturbances, relaxes in calm conditions — no observer needed.
Benchmark

Six Controllers Compared

A comprehensive side-by-side evaluation covering linear, sliding-mode, observer-based, and terminal-sliding-mode paradigms.

LQR
Linear Optimal
Bryson's rule tuning with integral augmentation on cross-track error. Systematic gain design for linearised dynamics.
SMC
Sliding Mode
Boundary-layer switching with fixed gains and model-based equivalent control. Inherent matched-disturbance robustness.
ASMC
Adaptive SMC
Ablation baseline: adds adaptive switching gain to SMC's linear surface without terminal dynamics or error-proportional leakage.
ADRC
Active Disturbance Rejection
2nd-order Extended State Observer estimates and cancels lumped disturbances. Model-free but observer-bandwidth limited.
FNTSMC
Fast Nonsingular Terminal SMC
Literature benchmark (Fan et al. 2021): non-singular terminal surface + finite-time disturbance observer + adaptive gain.
ANTSMC
Adaptive Terminal SMC
Proposed: fractional-power terminal surface + power-rate reaching + leakage-regulated adaptive gain. Observer-free design eliminates ESO bandwidth trade-offs.

Summary

Key Findings

Finding 01
Best Overall Controller
ANTSMC ranks #1 at SS2 and SS3 across all 6 controllers, and #2 at SS1 (within 4.3% of SMC).
Finding 02
Progressive Advantage
ANTSMC's improvement over baselines grows with disturbance intensity — designed precisely for when it matters most.
Finding 03
Observer-Free Design
Eliminates the ESO bandwidth trade-off that limits ADRC's transient performance at path corners and sharp manoeuvres.
Finding 04
MBZIRC Validated
Independent validation in a physics-based ROS2 maritime simulator confirms stable tracking across diverse path geometries.
Finding 05
Monte Carlo Stable
All 1,080 trials stable. ANTSMC leads at SS2 (6.0% over SMC) and SS3 (7.4% over SMC) with CV below 30%.
Finding 06
Ablation Validated
ASMC (adaptive gain only) and FNTSMC (terminal surface only) both underperform ANTSMC, confirming the synergy of combined mechanisms.
